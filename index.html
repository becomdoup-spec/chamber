The Problem

Cricket Gear Is a Breeding Ground

Sweat-soaked pads, gloves and helmets harbour dangerous levels of bacteria and fungi. Academies cycling 50–200 players daily face compounding hygiene risks that manual cleaning cannot address.

Slow Manual Process

Traditional sun-drying and wiping takes 4–6 hours per batch, leaving gear damp and partially sanitised between sessions.

Skin Infections

Shared equipment spreads ringworm, staph and fungal infections — the #1 complaint in residential cricket academies.

Equipment Degradation

Moisture trapped inside pads and gloves breaks down foam and stitching, cutting equipment lifespan by 30–40%.

High Replacement Cost

Academies spend ₹3–5 lakhs annually replacing gear that deteriorates from improper cleaning and storage.

The Solution

Five-Stage Chamber Cleaning Process

Each cycle follows a precisely timed sequence inside a sealed stainless-steel chamber. No manual intervention required after loading. Total cycle: 7 minutes 30 seconds.

01

Pre-Dust Extraction

0:00 – 1:00 1 min

High-velocity filtered air jets (HEPA H13) blast loose dirt, dried sweat crystals and particulate matter from all surfaces. A rotating rack ensures 360° coverage. Extracted debris is captured in a sealed collection tray.

Air Velocity20 m/s
Filter GradeHEPA H13 (99.95% @ 0.3µm)
Coverage360° rotational
02

UV-C Germicidal Irradiation

1:00 – 3:30 2 min 30 sec

254 nm UV-C lamps mounted on six axes irradiate every exposed surface. At close proximity inside the reflective chamber, irradiance reaches 2–5 mW/cm², delivering a cumulative dose of 300–750 mJ/cm² — far exceeding the 40 mJ/cm² required for 99.9% (3-log) bacterial elimination. The rotating rack eliminates shadow zones.

Wavelength254 nm (germicidal peak)
Dosage300–750 mJ/cm²
Lamps12 × 36W quartz tubes
03

Ozone Micro-Saturation

3:30 – 5:30 2 min

Ozone (O₃) at 0.1 ppm is diffused into the sealed chamber at elevated humidity (~70% RH from residual sweat moisture). Ozone penetrates deep into foam padding, fabric liners and crevices that UV-C cannot reach — oxidising embedded odour molecules and supplementing surface disinfection. All exposure occurs in an unmanned sealed chamber.

O₃ Concentration0.1 ppm (OSHA TWA limit)
PenetrationDeep-foam & crevices
Humidity~70% RH (enhanced efficacy)
04

Rapid Dehumidification

5:30 – 7:00 1 min 30 sec

Warm, dry air at 40°C — well within the safe range for all cricket equipment materials including leather, EVA foam and synthetic fabrics — circulates through the chamber. Residual ozone is catalytically converted back to O₂ via an MnO₂ catalyst bed. Surface moisture is reduced to below 15%.

Temperature40°C ± 2°C
Target Moisture< 15% surface
O₃ DecompositionMnO₂ catalytic
05

Anti-Microbial Nano-Mist

7:00 – 7:30 30 sec

A fine nano-mist of thymol-based (derived from thyme oil) anti-microbial solution is applied via ultrasonic atomisation at 5µm particle size. This invisible, non-toxic coating provides residual antimicrobial protection for 24–48 hours after treatment.

Particle Size5 µm (ultrasonic)
Active IngredientThymol (plant-derived)
Protection24–48 hours
